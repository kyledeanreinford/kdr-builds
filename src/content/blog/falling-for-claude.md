The first time I really got Claude Code hooked up and dialed in, it was a dream. I was able to create anything I wanted
in seconds. No need to plan, no need to take breaks - everything I needed was right there waiting for me to ask the
question and press "Accept". It's been great. I've written some cool apps and learned a ton about how to code in the
world of AI. But more and more my lunch breaks, evenings and weekends are pulled to my machine. The setup Claude has
with weekly and session limits makes it really tempting to jump in anytime. If you have to spread it out, when is it
your downtime?

I think I just realized it, actually.

Recently, I've been struggling to stay on top of home tasks. I've got a bunch of projects in the house and yard. I have
landscaping I'd like to do and laundry that must be done. Why, all the sudden, is that hard to keep up with? I'm
supposed to be able to do more than ever, thanks to AI. But the always-on temptation of creating whatever you want at
any time is too much for my willpower. Actually, I don't think it's even about willpower. Claude is a tool that can
build your dreams - why wouldn't you want to build as fast as possible? But one thing that brings up - when you build
something fast with AI, it's super easy to take it for granted. Easy to throw it away. Easy to forget you build it. It's
like how delayed gratification helps you enjoy your purchase, for instance. If you get it right away, you lose interest
sooner. I can build 15 apps a week with Claude if I want, but are any of the meaningful?
